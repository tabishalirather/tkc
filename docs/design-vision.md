# The Kashmir Co. Design Vision

## Purpose
Create a calm, premium, conversion-oriented commerce experience for Kashmiri essentials. The visual system should feel modern, trustworthy, and curated rather than decorative or dense.

## Core Philosophy
- Prefer subtraction over addition.
- Use fewer sections with stronger intent.
- Keep product discovery and purchase actions obvious.
- Build trust with concise proof, not long explanations.
- Keep a warm and natural tone rooted in Kashmiri origin.

## Visual Character
- Premium natural-products brand language.
- Warm, restrained color expression inspired by saffron, honey, walnut wood, cream, and muted green.
- Spacious layouts with deliberate white space.
- Soft borders and subtle shadows only where needed.
- Motion should be minimal and functional.
- Image-led storytelling with clean overlay text and concise commerce CTAs.

## Palette
Primary palette should reuse existing Tailwind tokens:
- Saffron: `saffron-500` for primary actions and key accents.
- Walnut: `walnut-900` to `walnut-600` for headings and body contrast.
- Cream: `cream` and `cream-50` for soft section backgrounds.
- Forest: `forest-700` to `forest-500` for positive states and secondary accents.

Usage rules:
- Keep most surfaces neutral (`white`, `cream-50`).
- Use saffron for CTA emphasis, not large background floods.
- Avoid high-saturation gradients on full sections.

## Typography
- Serif (`Playfair Display`) for high-impact headings and section titles.
- Sans (`Inter`) for body copy, metadata, and UI text.

Hierarchy rules:
- H1: concise, one clear promise.
- H2: short section labels focused on shopping intent.
- Body copy: 1-2 lines when possible.
- Avoid dense paragraphs on homepage.

## Spacing Principles
- Section spacing: `py-14` mobile, `py-20` desktop.
- Keep max text widths around `max-w-2xl` for readability.
- Card padding: `p-4` to `p-6` depending on content density.
- Preserve breathing room between heading, copy, and CTA.

## Radius and Border Rules
- Use `rounded-xl` or `rounded-2xl` for major cards and surfaces.
- Favor thin neutral borders (`border-walnut-100` or `border-saffron-200` for emphasis).
- Avoid heavy outlines and deeply nested containers.

## Buttons
Primary button:
- Solid saffron background.
- Strong contrast text.
- Reserved for main conversion actions.

Secondary button:
- Outline/neutral style.
- Used for contextual navigation (story, all products, etc.).

Button rules:
- One dominant CTA per section.
- Keep labels short and action-led.

## Cards and Surfaces
- Product and category cards should share a common structure:
  - image first
  - clear title
  - short support line
  - focused action
- Limit badge usage to one meaningful badge per card.
- Keep card effects subtle (`hover:shadow-sm`, tiny lift only).

## Background and Surface Treatment
- Alternate neutral sections (`white` and soft cream) for rhythm.
- No noisy texture overlays.
- No full-screen decorative hero effects.

## Icon Style
- Prefer simple line icons or minimal glyphs.
- Keep icon size restrained and purposeful.
- Avoid emoji-heavy trust or feature grids.

## Motion and Interaction
- Motion should support readability, not distract.
- Remove auto-rotating carousels where static content works.
- Use short transitions for hover/focus feedback.
- Avoid repeated bounce or floating animations.

## Homepage Section Rhythm
Recommended order:
1. Hero with clear value proposition and primary CTA.
2. Editorial category tiles (image-first).
3. Best sellers / featured products strip.
4. Trust pillars with concise proof.
5. Newsletter strip / final CTA.

Keep each section concise and avoid repeating the same message.

## Homepage Pattern Notes
- Use one full-bleed hero image with centered heading and one dominant CTA.
- Use editorial tile grids for category navigation (2-up and 4-up compositions).
- Keep product cards minimal: image, name, price, one action.
- Use a slim newsletter band rather than a large promotional block.
- Favor neutral gray canvases (`#efefed` family) with saffron accent links/buttons.

## Copywriting Tone
- Confident, calm, and factual.
- Short sentences and specific claims.
- Avoid exaggerated language and long storytelling blocks.
- Emphasize origin, quality checks, and practical customer value.

## Image Direction
- Clean, natural-light product photography.
- Warm color temperature and realistic textures.
- Keep framing uncluttered and product-first.
- Avoid overly stylized filters or loud collage layouts.

## Product Card Rules
- Show: image, name, price, optional rating.
- Optional: one badge and one discount marker.
- Keep descriptions short (`line-clamp-2`).
- Ensure add-to-cart remains immediately visible.

## Forms and Inputs
- Neutral, clear input fields with subtle border.
- Visible focus states (`saffron` ring/border).
- Keep forms compact and easy to complete.

## Responsive Behavior Principles
- Prioritize scanability on mobile.
- Stack content in a single column with clear spacing.
- Keep CTA buttons full width on smaller screens where appropriate.
- Avoid horizontal overflow and dense multi-column blocks on small viewports.

## Reuse Guidance for Other Pages
Apply this same system to:
- category and collection pages
- product detail pages
- cart and checkout
- account and order surfaces

The same design direction should hold: concise content, neutral surfaces, clean hierarchy, clear actions.

## Inspiration Note
This direction is inspired by premium minimalist commerce references such as Bellroy in terms of restraint, rhythm, and hierarchy, but it is adapted into an original Kashmiri premium-goods identity with its own tone, palette, and storytelling priorities.
